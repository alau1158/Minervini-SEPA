## Example 1: TRGP (Targa Resources) — April/May 2026

![TRGP Daily Chart](TRGP.png)

### Overview

Targa Resources is a strong Stage 2 trending stock with the **"VCP Pattern with Pocket Pivots by Mark Minervini"** indicator applied. Multiple pocket pivot signals (green triangles) fired throughout the trend. The question was whether an entry was valid around April 24–29, 2026.

### The Setup

- Clean Stage 2 uptrend — all SMAs stacked and rising from ~$175 to ~$267
- Multiple successful pocket pivot entries fired Feb through April
- April 24 pocket pivot fired near the 20 SMA — a valid signal in isolation
- Stock ran from ~$240 to ~$267 after the April 24 signal

### The Scenario (Hypothetical: Tuesday April 28)

| Day | Price Action | Volume | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Friday (prior) | Tight, narrow range | Low / drying up | VCP pivot completing ✅ |
| Monday 4/28 | Red candle, **below 20 SMA** | **High volume** | 🔴 Distribution signal |
| Tuesday | Recovered green close | Moderate | Encouraging but not enough |
| Wednesday 6:30am | Evaluating entry | — | Decision point |

### Why This Is a Pass

- **20 SMA was breached on Monday** — non-negotiable disqualifier under SEPA
- **High volume on the red day** = institutional distribution, not a shakeout
- Tuesday's recovery does not erase a high-volume distribution day
- Stop placement becomes ambiguous — below Monday's low? Below 20 SMA? Below Tuesday's low?
- When you can't define your stop cleanly, the trade doesn't qualify

### The Wednesday Morning Decision

Even with Tuesday closing green, Minervini would **not enter Wednesday morning** because:

1. Monday's 20 SMA breach on high volume = pattern compromised
2. One green recovery day is insufficient to confirm accumulation
3. Pre-set buy stop would not have been placed Tuesday night given the breach
4. "If in doubt, stay out" — the doubt is justified here

### What Needs to Happen for a Valid Re-Entry

| Requirement | Why |
|---|---|
| Build a new tighter base | Reset the VCP clock properly |
| Volume dry up for several more days | Confirm selling pressure exhausted |
| 20 SMA catch back up to price | Restore proper SMA alignment |
| New clean pivot high on quiet volume | Fresh, uncompromised entry point |

### Conclusion

> **PASS.** The 20 SMA breach on high volume disqualifies this as a clean SEPA entry. A compromised VCP with ambiguous stop placement is not a trade — it's a gamble. Wait for a clean reset.

**Key Lesson:** A trade you didn't take on a compromised setup isn't a missed opportunity — it's a bullet dodged.

## Example 2: BIIB (Biogen) — May 2026

![BIIB Daily Chart](BIIB.png)

### Overview

Biogen appeared to form a pivot around May 4–5, 2026, with a pocket pivot signal firing and a breakout attempt to ~$204. However, a large red candle appeared shortly after. The question: shakeout or distribution? And does the pattern qualify under SEPA at all?

### Base Quality Assessment (Jan – April 2026)

| Element | Observation |
|---|---|
| Base structure | ❌ Wide, choppy, erratic — not orderly contractions |
| SMA alignment | ❌ 20 and 50 SMA tangled and flat Feb–March |
| Volume behavior | ❌ Large spikes mid-base — not quiet drying up |
| Stage classification | ⚠️ Stage 1 basing / transition, not clean Stage 2 |
| Handle tightness | ⚠️ Only 1–2 weeks tight after months of chop |

**The base was already disqualified before the red candle appeared.**

### The May 4–5 Pivot

- Price compressed around $180–185
- Volume quieter in the pivot zone
- Pocket pivot indicator fired (green triangle)
- Breakout attempted to ~$204 (+~13%)

However: a 1–2 week tight handle on top of a months-long messy base is **not a textbook VCP** — it's a short handle on a poor foundation.

### The Big Red Candle

| Factor | Observation | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Still above 20 SMA | ✅ Held | Positive |
| Volume | 🔴 2.8M — one of largest bars on chart | Distribution |
| Candle size | 🔴 Large range, significant selling | Distribution |
| Prior base quality | 🔴 Already wide and messy | Negative context |
| Stage | ⚠️ Transition, not clean Stage 2 | Caution |

### Shakeout or Distribution?

**This is distribution, not a shakeout.** Here's why:

- A true shakeout = moderate volume, quick recovery, knocks out weak hands
- 2.8M volume on a large red candle = **institutional selling**, not weak-hand shaking
- Stock ran +13% then reversed hard on massive volume = **climactic reversal signal**
- The 20 SMA hold is necessary but not sufficient given the volume context

### Conclusion

> **PASS.** The base never met SEPA quality standards to begin with. The high-volume red candle confirms distribution. The 20 SMA hold keeps the door slightly open for a future setup, but this is a watchlist-only situation — not an entry.

**What to watch for:** If BIIB can consolidate quietly above the 20 SMA for 2–3 more weeks with volume truly drying up, a cleaner setup could emerge. Until then — hands off.

## The Big Picture Lesson: Steps vs. Gorges

Minervini wants **stair-steps**, not a hiking trail with valleys and gorges.

| ✅ What You Want (Steps) | ❌ What You Don't Want (Gorges) |
|---|---|
| Clean, orderly stair-steps up | Jagged valleys and erratic swings |
| Each pullback shallower than the last | Wild swings up and down |
| Volume quietly drying up in base | Huge volume spikes mid-base |
| 20/50 SMA cleanly stacked like a ribbon | SMAs tangled and crossing each other |
| Tight, controlled corrections | Deep, erratic selloffs |

### The 5-Second Rule

If you can't tell within **5 seconds** that it's a clean uptrend with orderly pullbacks — move on. Minervini rejects most charts in under 10 seconds. The right ones are obvious.

**Quick checklist:**
- Can I draw a clean rising channel? → Steps ✅
- Are the SMAs stacked neatly like a ribbon? → Steps ✅
- Do the pullbacks look calm and orderly? → Steps ✅
- Does it look like a heart monitor or a mountain range? → Gorges ❌
